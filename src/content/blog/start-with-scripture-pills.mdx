If [threads](/blog/start-with-threads) are the first thing I’d show a new user, scripture pills are a close second.

A scripture pill is the little reference chip on a note—Romans 8:1, Psalm 23, John 15:5—that ties what you wrote to the text it came from.

It sounds small. It’s the difference between a journal and a <mark>working library</mark>.

---

## Why pills matter

Without a reference, your note is only as findable as its title.

With a pill, you can come back through the passage. Sermon next month on the same text? Your old sentence is waiting. Teaching prep that needs “everything I’ve ever written on grace in Paul”? The references do the sorting you won’t remember to do by hand.

<mark>The verse becomes the index.</mark> That’s the whole product idea in one chip.

---

## How to use them without fuss

- Add the pill while the thought is hot—don’t “tidy later”
- One primary reference is enough; add more only when the note truly spans texts
- Don’t invent a reference to feel organized. If it’s a theme note, put it in a thread and pill the verses as they show up

Pills and threads work together. The thread is the journey. The pill is the pin on the map.

---

## A two-minute habit

After you read, write the three-line capture ([five-minute version here](/blog/the-five-minute-capture) if you want the fuller rhythm). Put a pill on the note before you close the app.

That’s it. No tagging taxonomy. No folder tree.

---

If you’re already living in threads, open one note today and make sure the pill is there. Future-you is the person you’re serving.
