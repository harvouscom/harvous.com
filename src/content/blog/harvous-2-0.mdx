For years, Harvous grew two faces on the web: Classic — spaces, threads, and a dashboard hierarchy — and a prototype shell that felt more like the Mac and iOS apps. Keeping both meant twice the navigation rules, twice the "where do I click?" moments, and a product story that was hard to explain in one sentence.

**Harvous 2.0 ends that split.**

As of this release, the native-like shell is the only signed-in web experience. Classic URLs redirect. Your notes, highlights, folders, and scripture pills are the same rows in the database — we changed the home screen, not your library.

---

## What you will notice

**Note-first URLs.** You open a note, not a dashboard. The sidebar lists what matters for My Home: notes, folders, highlights, and scripture.

**Folders instead of thread piles.** Classic organizational threads became folders on first visit. Study threads still exist — but for connected notes you are actively tracing, not for "where does this file live?"

**A calmer Home.** Today's passage, gentle recall suggestions, and a founder letter when you are new. Tap "Not now" on anything you do not need today.

**Parity with native.** Glass sidebar, inspector, bottom study docks — the same patterns you already know on Mac or iPhone.

---

## What did not change

- Clerk sign-in and your account
- Note content, encryption, and sharing links
- Spaces data (shared spaces flows still use their dedicated join paths)
- The conviction behind the product: scripture-linked notes you can find again

If something feels missing, check **folders**, the **inspector**, or **Settings** before assuming it is gone.

---

## Version 2.0.0

We reset public versioning at this milestone. The long `1.217.x` build numbers were an internal patch train while the prototype matured. **2.0.0** marks Classic retirement (June 30, 2026) and the shell you see today.
