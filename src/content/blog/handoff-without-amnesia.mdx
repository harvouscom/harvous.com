Someone good is stepping down from the class. Or the group. Or the Wednesday night series.

They’re tired in the faithful way. They loved the people. They also have a folder of notes nobody else can decode—and a head full of context that will walk out with them unless you ask for it.

This is the quiet crisis of church education: <mark>handoffs erase continuity</mark> even when the people were thriving.

---

## What walks out with them

Not the curriculum brand. The living knowledge:

- Which questions opened *this* room
- Which passages still feel unfinished
- Who is new, who’s carrying grief, who teaches sideways with good questions
- What you already tried that flopped

A syllabus doesn’t hold that. A spreadsheet of attendance doesn’t either.

---

## The one-page handoff

Ask the outgoing leader for one page (or one note) before the last Sunday. Not a memoir. Four blocks:

**Where we are.** Passage range + series aim in one sentence.

**What stuck.** Two moments the room actually remembered.

**What’s open.** One question or tension for the next leader to honor, not ignore.

**Who to watch for.** Two pastoral notes (with care, not gossip): someone new, someone hurting, someone ready to help.

If they already use a [lesson-prep stack](/blog/the-lesson-prep-stack), half of this is already written.

---

## Don’t hand off only files

Sending “here’s the Drive” feels responsible. It usually isn’t enough.

Sit for twenty minutes. Walk the one-pager together. Let the new leader ask dumb questions while the old leader still remembers the answers.

Tools help here only as memory aids. Humans still transfer the care.

---

## For the church side

Staff can normalize this without building a bureaucracy:

- Put “handoff note” on the calendar when a leader’s last month begins
- Keep handoff notes in the same place series trails live
- Thank people for the trail, not only for showing up

When churches talk systems, start with continuity people can feel—see [what church education systems need](/blog/what-church-education-systems-need)—not with another login.

---

If you’re building toward shared study spaces later, the habit still matters now: personal prep that leaves a trail is what any future group layer will inherit. [Teachers](/for/teachers/) and [group leaders](/for/group-leaders/) already need that primitive.
